Machine Learning for EM Duct Characterization Within the Marine Atmosphere

Utilizing machine learning approaches to tackle real-time characterization of ducting phenomena within the marine atmosphere

Advisor: Christopher J. Earls

The performance of communication systems and radars can be heavily impacted by atmospheric ducting, a common environmental phenomena that can cause EM signals to refract back toward the ocean surface. The goal of this research is to discover a real-time method to characterize these ducts (i.e. find values of important parameters that describe the ducts' effect on the refractivity of the region) to help operators make adjustments, in order to minimize their effect.
